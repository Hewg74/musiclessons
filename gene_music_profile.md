## Interpretive Analysis — What This Data Actually Means

### The Listening Archetype
Gene is a **"Coastal Psychedelic Omnivore"** — someone whose musical center of gravity is the overlap between California/Australian surf-psych scenes and Caribbean/Pacific reggae traditions, with tendrils reaching into West African desert blues, Latin American rhythms, instrumental soul-funk, and ambient/neoclassical territories.

### Geographic-Scene Mapping
His taste maps to specific *scenes*, not just genres:
- **LA Psych-Surf:** Allah-Las, Mystic Braves, levitation room, The Undercover Dream Lovers, GHOSTWOMAN
- **Australian Surf-Garage:** Babe Rainbow, Sun Room, Surf Hat, Surf Trash, Ocean Alley, DOPE LEMON, Royel Otis, Rum Jungle, Marlin's Dreaming
- **SoCal Reggae-Rock:** Pepper, Slightly Stoopid, Iration, The Hip Abduction, Fortunate Youth, The Green
- **Global/World Fusion:** Khruangbin (Thai-funk-soul), Tinariwen (Tuareg desert blues), Hermanos Gutiérrez (Ecuadorian cinematic guitar), Kokoroko (Afrobeat-jazz), Rosinha De Valença (Brazilian bossa)
- **UK/Euro Psych-Soul:** Skinshape, Babeheaven, Camel Power Club, Widowspeak
- **Instrumental/Ambient:** Tommy Guerrero (skate-soul-jazz), Joe Hisaishi (Ghibli neoclassical), Erland Cooper (ambient/classical), Sapien Medicine (The Flow)

### Sonic Signature — What Recommendations Should Sound Like
- **Tempo:** Predominantly mid-tempo (90-120 BPM). Very little fast punk energy or slow ballad territory. The sweet spot is "you could either surf or sway to this."
- **Production:** Warm, analog-leaning, reverb-drenched. Lo-fi over hi-fi. Spring reverb, tape saturation, and room mics over surgical digital clarity.
- **Instrumentation:** Guitar-forward (especially jangly, surf-tremolo, and nylon-string). Prominent bass lines (often reggae-influenced). Organ/keys as texture, not lead. Horns welcomed (Kokoroko, The Hip Abduction style).
- **Vocals:** Laid-back delivery preferred. No belting, no screaming, no overwrought R&B runs. Think stoned-afternoon-on-the-porch register.
- **Mood:** Perpetual golden hour. Sun-drenched, slightly melancholic, nostalgic but not sad. "Beautiful day that you know will end" energy.
- **Lyrical themes:** Nature, ocean, travel, love (but not melodramatic). Minimal angst. No nihilism, no party-bro energy.

### What NOT to Recommend
- Mainstream pop, EDM, or Top 40 (Joel Corry at #50 is the exception, not the rule)
- Heavy metal, hardcore punk, or aggressive genres
- Overly polished/modern pop production (Billie Eilish, The Weeknd territory)
- Country (mainstream Nashville)
- Hip-hop (unless it's beat-tape/instrumental like Cap Kendricks, Moderator style)
- Emo, post-punk revival, or shoegaze with heavy distortion
